Food Gallery

Tempura is a classical Portuguese dish brought to and popularized by Japan, consisting of seafood or vegetables that have been battered and deep fried.

Tempura

Mochi is Japanese rice cake made of mochigome, a short-grain japonica glutinous rice. The rice is pounded into paste and molded into the desired shape.

Mochi

Onigiri also known as o-musubi, nigirimeshi or rice ball, is a Japanese food made from white rice formed into triangular or cylindrical shapes and often wrapped in nori.

Onigiri

Takoyaki is a ball-shaped Japanese snack made of a wheat flour-based batter and cooked in a special moulded pan.

Takoyaki

Dango is a Japanese dumpling and sweet made from mochiko, related to mochi. It is often served with green tea.

Dango

Wakame Salad Japanese seaweed salads are made with Hiyashi Wakame, and are sometimes referred to as Goma Wakame

Wakame Salad

Melon Pan A melonpan is a type of sweet bun from Japan, that is also popular in Taiwan, China and Latin America.

Melon Pan

Korokke is the Japanese name for a deep-fried dish originally related to a French dish, the croquette.

Korokke

Kamaboko is a type of cured surimi, a Japanese processed seafood product.

Kamaboko

Inarizushi is a kind of sushi or rice ball. Sushi rice is stuffed in seasoned Aburaage tofu pouches.

Inarizushi

Miso Soup is a traditional Japanese soup consisting of a stock called "dashi" into which softened miso paste is mixed.

Miso Soup

Oshiruko Red bean soup or porridge refers to various traditional Asian soups or porridge made with azuki beans.

Oshiruko
